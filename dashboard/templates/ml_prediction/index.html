Recommendations: How to Use Predictions

Inventory Planning

Use the 95% confidence intervals to plan inventory levels. Stock according to the upper bound to avoid stockouts, or use the point estimate for lean inventory management.

Pricing Strategy

Test different price points to see predicted demand changes. Find the optimal price-demand balance by comparing predicted revenue across various price levels.

Strategic Planning

Compare predictions across regions and retailers to identify high-potential markets. Use seasonal trends (Quarter/Month) for campaign timing and promotional planning.

Interpreting Confidence Levels
  • High Confidence (75%+): Prediction is highly reliable. Model has strong certainty based on similar historical patterns.
  • Medium Confidence (50-75%): Prediction is reasonable but consider the confidence interval range for planning.
  • Low Confidence (<50%): Prediction has higher uncertainty. Use cautiously and prepare contingency plans.

Model Context & Performance Insights
Context: Understanding the ML Model

Model Architecture: This demand forecasting system uses a Random Forest Regressor algorithm, an ensemble learning method that combines multiple decision trees to produce accurate predictions.

Training Data: The model was trained on 9,648 historical sales records from Adidas US, spanning multiple retailers, regions, product categories, and sales channels.

Purpose: Predict future product demand (units sold) and revenue based on market conditions, helping stakeholders make informed decisions about inventory management, pricing strategies, and resource allocation.

Key Findings: Model Performance
Revenue Predictions
  • 82.8% accuracy (R²) - The model explains 82.8% of revenue variance
  • $3,139 average error (MAE) - Only 3.4% of mean revenue ($93,273)
  • 35× better than baseline - Significantly outperforms naive predictions
Units Predictions
  • 75.1% accuracy (R²) - Excellent variance explanation for unit forecasting
  • 68 units average error (MAE) - Minimal deviation from actual demand
  • Robust predictions - Consistent performance across product categories
Most Influential Factors

The model identified Product Category, Retailer Type, Price per Unit, and Geographic Region as the most significant predictors of demand. Seasonal patterns (Quarter, Month) also play a crucial role in forecasting accuracy.
